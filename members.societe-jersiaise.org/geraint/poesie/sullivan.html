Jean Sullivan - John Sullivan, "J.S.", "Oméga"

1813-1899
  

 

Jean Sullivan wrote in three languages - Jèrriais, French and English. He was also a notary, journalist, historian, publisher of pamphlets, and devoted monarchist who always claimed the patronage of the Queen. He was a self-publicist who had several brushes with the law, an opinionated and indefatigable writer of letters to newspapers, and has also been called Jersey's answer to William McGonagall.

He corresponded with Victor Hugo - and may have known him socially, as he did Tennyson (who visited his brother in Jersey on occasion). He worked with Millais, and moved in the highest cultural circles of Jersey life. He lectured and published on history, and his print of the Royal Square in the past still crops up as an illustration of life in St. Helier temps passé.

He promoted the Jersey language (using his own system of spelling) as well as writing in French and English. Make up your own mind on his merits as a poet, by following the links and reading some of his poems.

Nécrologie

Mort de John Sullivan, Ecr.

Nous regrettons d'apprendre que John Sullivan, écr., Notaire public, est décédé à son domicile "Clarendon House", 70, St. John's Road, Jeudi dernier, à l'âge de 86 ans. Le défunt avait été Capitaine dans la Milice et pendant quelques années Rédacteur et propriétaire du journal Jersey Observer. Il était aussi poëte Jersiais, d'assez de talent et avait publié plusieurs gravures, notamment la Place du Vier Marchi.

Chronique de Jersey 5/8/1899

Le Jersiais 9/7/1842

 

 


Comme il a couru une version différente touchant le déjeûner que M. Jean Sullivan fit prendre Samedi dernier, vers les huit heures du matin, à F.-A. Romeril, le rédacteur du Jersiais et de l'Impartial, nous rétablissons les faits.....

... M. J. Sullivan porta un coup de poing à F.-A. Romeril, qui le fit tomber dans un des carreaux de vitre du bureau de Jean Aubin, jun., écr., et il lui administra ensuite une bonne correction avec un fouet, le chassant devant lui, en même temps, jusqu'à la maison de M. Worton, perruquier, Morier-lane. Pendant cette correction, Romeril fuyait en demandant grace, et en criant aux personnes présentes: Sauvez-moi!...

John Sullivan - notary, writer, poet, artist and publisher

With reference to Mr. R.C. Harris, of South Africa and his print of “Le Vier Marchi” by John Sullivan, the latter was one of Jersey's most prominent characters in Queen Victoria's reign.

A notary, writer, poet, artist and publisher, with several other activities, he was an ardent Jersey loyalist and his sketches and writings were usually “Dedicated by special permission to Her Majesty Queen Victorai” of “Patronized by Her Majesty.”

Among his numerous publications in English or Norman French were “Jersey's Jubilee Souvenir of our Gracious Queen,” “The Norman Conquest and the Crusades,” “Haro Haro Haro,” “Le Drapeau du Jubilé,” and “Lieut.-Governor Don.”

Several engravings and sketches by John Sullivan can still be seen in Jersey (the Société Jersiaise Museum included) and they are all noteworthy for the imaginative character which Sullivan displayed in his various activities....

G.G.H. Le C.

Evening Post 25/9/1965
